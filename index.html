Internet Infrastructure in Karori Rd, Wellington

Jim Guo





As the need for social development, Internet infrastructure is becoming more important for supporting residents’ daily life. Karori is one of New Zealand's biggest suburbs located at the western edge of Wellington. With a population of over 14,000 settling down here, some Internet infrastructures can be found along on its main road, Karori road.

Antenna

Most antennas are set for residents to transmit or receive the video and audio signals of television broadcasts. These antennas are mounted on a mast on top of the owner's house.

ATM

There are two ATMs on the Karori road. ATM is an electronic telecommunications device that enables customers to withdraw cash, deposit, or transfer fund without the need for bank staffs.

Booth

A telephone booth is a small structure equipped with a public telephone.

Bus-stop Electronic Timetable

Bus-stop Electronic Timetable is up-to-the minute information on when a bus or train service is due to arrive at your stop or station. It is based on the actual locations of all buses, which are constantly monitored by GPS. This information is used to predict arrival times reflecting actual conditions at the time.

cable

Network cables are used to connect and transfer data and information between computers, routers, switches and storage area networks. These cables are essentially the carrier or media through which data flows.

CCTV

It is a TV system in which signals are not publicly distributed but are monitored, primarily for surveillance and security purposes.

Manhole cover

The underground network goes through manholes of various sizes – some are simply a small access pit in the local street, while others are really large cable chambers.

Powerline

It carries electrical power to pass different areas or buildings and support internet infrastructure.

Traffic Light

They are computer-controlled and internet-connected to control the movement of traffic.
